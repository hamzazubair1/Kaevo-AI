# Kaevo AI — Standard Operating Procedures (SOPs)

> Master index and standards for all Kaevo AI SOPs. Every process that is performed more than once must have an SOP.

---

## SOP Standards

### What is an SOP?
A Standard Operating Procedure is a step-by-step guide that ensures any team member can perform a task correctly, consistently, and independently — without needing to ask someone else.

### SOP Template

Every SOP must follow this structure:

```markdown
# SOP: [Process Name]

**Version:** 1.0
**Last Updated:** YYYY-MM-DD
**Owner:** [Name/Role]
**Category:** [Engineering/Client/Operations/Deployment]

## Purpose
Why this SOP exists and what it accomplishes.

## Scope
What this SOP covers and what it doesn't.

## Prerequisites
What you need before starting.

## Procedure
1. Step one
2. Step two
3. Step three

## Expected Outcome
What the result should look like.

## Troubleshooting
Common issues and how to resolve them.

## Revision History
| Version | Date | Author | Changes |
|---------|------|--------|---------|
| 1.0 | YYYY-MM-DD | Name | Initial version |
```

## SOP Lifecycle

```
Draft → Review → Approved → Active → Revision → Re-Approved
```

1. **Draft** — Author writes the SOP
2. **Review** — Team reviews for accuracy and completeness
3. **Approved** — Lead approves for use
4. **Active** — SOP is in use
5. **Revision** — Updates needed (process change, improvement)
6. **Re-Approved** — Updated version approved

---

## Rules

1. Every process performed more than twice must have an SOP
2. SOPs must be updated whenever the process changes
3. SOPs must be written clearly enough for a new team member to follow
4. Include screenshots and examples where possible
5. Assign an owner to every SOP
6. Review all SOPs quarterly

---

> **Location:** All SOPs are stored in the `sops/` directory, organized by category.
